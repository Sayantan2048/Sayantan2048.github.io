I am a Compter Graphics student at McGill University. I joined McGill in September, 2016 and before that I worked three years in a Power Plant working in various roles including instrumentation(primary), networking and security and web development. I completed my Bachelor's in 2013 in Electronics and Communication Engineering from National Institute of Technology, Durgapur, India. I had interest in graphics since I was an undergrad when I taught myself old OpenGL and also enjoyed the EdX Course in Computer Graphics by Prof. Ramamoorthi. I also have this weird fascination for GPUs - probably because I couldn't play Crysis on my PC during my school days :P. So, I learned OpenCL and started contributing towards open source project John-The-Ripper where I built several GPU accelarated cryptography algorithms.

My current research:
  • Building friction model from microfacet distribution. This is also my Master's thesis. Since the origin of friction on meso-scale(order of mm) is due to the surface geometry which is also related to microfacet distribution used in path tracing, we are trying to build a model which behaves both physically and visually as per the surface geometry.
  • Effect of control variates in path tracing. We are exploring the effects of analytic and non-analytic integrators as control variate with the aim of reducing variance and improving convergence in path tracing and global illumination.

My other intersts are:
  • Exploring Deep Learning and Reinforcement Learning methods for noise/variance reduction in path tracing and character animation.
  • GPU programming and shader development.
